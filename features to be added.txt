Since we've just laid down the groundwork for allowing the system to handle multiple educational levels (Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary), there are a few really interesting directions we can take this to make it feel like a fully polished, multi-level institution management system. 

Here are my top suggestions for what we could tackle next:

### 1. Parent/Guardian Portal (Highly Recommended)
Primary and Secondary school students rarely pay their own fees; parents do. We could build a feature where a user registers as a "Parent/Guardian", and they can "link" multiple student accounts to their profile. This way, a parent with one kid in Primary and another in Secondary can log in once and see a consolidated dashboard of both their children's fee balances and payment deadlines.

### 2. Admin Analytics by School Level
Right now, your Admin Dashboard shows the total revenue across the entire institution. We could break this down! We can add a segmented chart or filtering toggles to the admin dashboard so administrators can immediately see the financial health isolated by "Primary", "Secondary", or "Tertiary" independently.

### 3. Student Management Filtering
In the Admin's Student Management list, we can add a quick-filter tab system ("All" | "Primary" | "Secondary" | "Tertiary") so that front-end staff can easily manage and search through students based on the school they fall under without them all getting mixed together.

### 4. Customizing Generated Documents (Receipts/Clearance Letters)
We can update the PDF generation engine so that if a student is universally cleared, their clearance letter or payment receipts visibly state "Primary School Department" vs "University", perhaps even with slightly different letterheads or text phrasing.
